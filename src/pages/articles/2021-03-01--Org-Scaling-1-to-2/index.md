## Zero to One

So, you are a small, nimble, self selected, high performance team that has built a product from zero to one. 
Over the last many months, the team has fought many fires, built features at breakneck speed without sacrificing quality.
Most of the time, high level feature requirements would be discussed directly one-on-one and large chunks of work would be accomplished  because of high bandwidth communication established by demonstrating competence and trust.

This is the zero to one phase where the product establishes itself, finds its footing in the market.

### Must Have Traits
In a team at this phase, it is critical to have people who embody the following traits.

* Founder mindset vs 9-to-5 mindset
* Problem finding skills
* Proactive Doer
* Craftsmanship and passion for excellence


## One to Two

 This is the next phase where the company has validated the product with early customers and is ready for bigger things. This coincides with Series A funding.

At this stage it cannot work the same way as before. There needs to be stability and to de-risk dependency on individuals who were otherwise owning entire modules or feature-sets entirely.

There are more customers getting onboarded, the features need to be enhanced to handle more scenarios, the UX needs to rethought and professional product managers take over for day to day work from founders.

This is the first time the otherwise self organizing team is getting organized under an org hierarchy, roles and middle management and it is crucial to get it right because this transition establishes the 2-to-n phases later on.

Over the last few consulting gigs, I have come worked in orgs that have gone through this same process - i.e. transition from a zero-to-one org to a one-to-two org and have come to observe common mistakes in their journey.
